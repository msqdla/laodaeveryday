《十日谈》第一日（英文原文 + 中文权威译文）

English excerpts: J. M. Rigg (1903, public domain). Chinese excerpts: 上海译文出版社 译本（方平、王科一 等）。点击链接进入每个故事的专页。
# English (Rigg, 1903) 中文引文（上译社） Link
1 Ser Cepparello by a false confession deceives a holy friar, and dies; and having been reputed a saint, is called San Ciappelletto. “宣扬恰泼莱托的一生奇迹，把他的斋戒、童贞、清白和圣洁等等都讲到了。” story_01.html
2 Abraham, a Jew, at the instance of Jehannot de Chevigné, goes to the Court of Rome, and seeing the evil life of the clergy, yet, returning, becomes a Christian. “不管他们怎样拼命想把天主教推翻，它可还是屹然不动，倒反而日益发扬光大。” story_02.html
3 Melchisedech the Jew with a story of three rings avoids a parlous trap laid for him by Saladin. “那三只戒指十分相象，确是分辨不出哪一只是假的来。” story_03.html
4 A monk lapses into sin, being allured by a woman, and is rescued from death by the abbot’s promptitude, who takes his place. “一个小修士犯了戒律，理应受到重罚；他却使用巧计，证明院长也犯了这个过失，因之逃过了责罚。” story_04.html
5 The Marchioness of Montferrat, by a banquet of hens seasoned with witty words, checks the mad passion of the King of France. “夫人，难道这里全是母鸡，公鸡一只也没有吗？” story_05.html
6 A worthy man by an apt speech puts to shame the wicked hypocrisy of the religious orders. “一个正直的人用一句尖刻得体的话，把修士的虚伪嘲笑得体无完肤。” story_06.html
7 Bergamino, with a story of Primasso and the Abbot of Cluny, neatly reproves a sudden avarice in Messer Cane della Scala. “贝加密诺讲述一个‘泼里马索和克伦尼院长’的故事，借题讽刺了一个贵族的近来的吝啬作风。” story_07.html
8 Guglielmo Borsiere by a neat retort sharply censures avarice in Messer Ermino de’ Grimaldi. “行吟诗人波西厄尔用一句锋利的话教导了一个老吝啬，叫他弃恶从善。” story_08.html
9 The King of Cyprus, being derided by a lady, is by a retort of hers stirred to a generous anger, and rewards her worthily. “塞浦路斯岛的国王昏庸无能，受了一位太太的讽刺，从此变得英明有为。” story_09.html
10 Master Alberto da Bologna handsomely puts to shame a lady who thought to have shamed him for being enamoured of her. “一位才德兼备的君子的爱情总是值得珍重的。” story_10.html
